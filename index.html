Withdrawal sequencing

1. Taxable — cap gains first

Draw from brokerage first — long-term capital gains taxed at 0/15/20%. Step-up basis resets on death. Preserves tax-advantaged growth in IRA/Roth.

2. Traditional IRA — bracket-managed

Strategic Roth conversions and draws to fill lower brackets. Avoid large RMDs that push into 32%+ brackets. IRS mandates RMDs at age 73+ regardless of need.

3. Roth — last resort

Tax-free growth, no RMDs, tax-free to heirs. Touch last. Every year of compound growth in Roth is permanently tax-free.

4. DCP / NQDC — mandatory installments

DCP drawn over 10 installments starting at retirement. Taxed as ordinary income. Portfolio dividends (~2% of taxable) are also mandatory each year. Both non-discretionary — coordinate with bracket planning.

Estate optimization

Taxable — step-up basis

Heirs receive assets at FMV on date of death. All embedded capital gains eliminated. Direct indexing gives more granular step-up on every individual lot.

Roth — tax-free inheritance

Beneficiaries inherit tax-free but must empty within 10 years (SECURE Act). No income tax on any distributions. Ideal for high-income heirs.

Traditional — convert & drain

Traditional IRA inherited by non-spouse must be emptied within 10 years, all taxed as ordinary income. Convert as much as practical to Roth during retirement.

Estate exemption

2025 federal estate exemption: $15M per person ($30M married). Above this, 40% estate tax. Plan with trusts and gifting strategies.
