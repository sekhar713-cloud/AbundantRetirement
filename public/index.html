The walkthrough
Seven steps from "what is this thing" to "I have a plan"

Follow these in order on your first visit. After that, return whenever something changes — markets moved, you got a raise, you discovered artisanal cheese subscriptions are apparently a retirement budget category.

1
My Portfolio tab
Tell it what you've got

Click Enter My Own Holdings and start adding your ETFs. Type a ticker — VOO, AVUV, BND, SCHD, whatever — and it will auto-fill the fund name, expense ratio, and category. Set the account type for each holding (Taxable, Traditional, or Roth). Then enter your dollar balances. These numbers are the engine of everything else.

💡 Or pick a model portfolio. If staring at your 14-ETF spaghetti allocation feels like too much before coffee, choose the Factor Portfolio, S&P Blend, or Merriman Worldwide and come back to the custom stuff later. We don't judge. Bogle wouldn't either.
⬇ Hit "Activate This Portfolio" when done
2
Liquid Net Worth tab
See the full arc of your financial life

This chart shows your taxable, Traditional, and Roth balances year by year from today to age 100. A gold marker sits on your retirement date. Watch the bars grow during accumulation, peak at retirement, and (hopefully) stay above zero for the next 40–50 years.

😬 If the bars hit zero at age 73, that's not a feature — that's a very urgent suggestion to revisit your burn rate. The chart does not soften this news.
3
Location Alpha tab
Get your "wrong house, wrong neighborhood" fix

This is where FIRE Portal checks whether your funds are living in the right accounts. Bonds in a taxable account are like wearing a wool sweater in August — technically functional, but you are paying an entirely avoidable cost every single year. The tool gives you a fund-by-fund table with specific relocation recommendations and the tax logic behind each one.

🏠 The typical gain from optimizing location is 0.5%–1.5% per year after-tax, with zero change in your risk profile. Compounded over 20 years, that's usually six figures. The reallocation itself takes an afternoon and a few clicks in your brokerage.
⬇ Check the Tax Alpha tab next to see the dollar cost of inaction
4
Burn Stages tab
Confess what you'll actually spend

Most retirement calculators ask for one spending number and then use it for 30 years. This is fiction. Real retirement spending looks like a smile: higher early (travel! experiences! the boat you definitely don't need!), quieter in the middle, then higher again late because healthcare has opinions.

Model three phases — Active, Moderate, and Late — with per-category detail. Be honest. The Monte Carlo simulator will find out if you're not.

🚨 "I'll just spend $4,000 a month, it'll be fine" — said every person before they discovered that international business class is $6,400 one-way and they are, at this point, too old to pretend economy is comfortable. Build the real number.
5
Retirement tab
Dial in your withdrawal parameters

Set your retirement age, horizon (up to 60 years — FIRE Portal was built knowing that retiring at 48 means you need a very long runway), and inflation regime. Add your guaranteed income: Social Security, pension, rental income. Choose your Social Security claim age — the difference between claiming at 62 vs. 70 is 24% more income per month, permanently. Forever. The math is not subtle.

The CAPE adjustment

The Bengen SAFEMAX rate (the famous 4% rule) is adjusted for current market valuations using the Shiller CAPE ratio. When stocks are expensive relative to earnings, the tool nudges your safe rate downward. It won't flatter you. It will keep you solvent.

6
Monte Carlo tab
Run 1,000 futures and see which ones survive

Click Run 1,000 Simulations. The tool generates 1,000 random return sequences — including sequences that have never occurred in recorded history — and runs your actual burn-stage spending plan against all of them. The result is a survival probability and a distribution of ending balances.

🎲 Monte Carlo can generate scenarios worse than the Great Depression combined with the 1970s stagflation followed by another crash, all in sequence. It is not trying to depress you. It is trying to build the kind of confidence that doesn't crumble the first time the S&P drops 20%.
SAFEMAX vs. Monte Carlo — they're measuring different things

100% SAFEMAX historical survival ≠ 100% Monte Carlo survival. That's expected, not a bug. SAFEMAX says every sequence that ever happened ended fine. Monte Carlo says if the future includes new sequences, here's your probability. Read both. Trust both. Panic about neither.

7
Export JSON
Save your work. It lives in your browser, not our servers.

Hit ↓ Export JSON at the top of the page. This downloads a single file containing your entire plan — portfolio, balances, burn stages, all settings. Save it somewhere sensible. Next time you open FIRE Portal, hit ↑ Import JSON and pick up exactly where you left off.

🔒 There is no account. There is no cloud. There is no server that knows you exist. Your retirement numbers belong to you. The JSON file is your plan. Guard it like you guard your Roth — zealously, and with a long time horizon.
⬇ Return whenever markets move, life changes, or you invent a new budget category
The three-alpha framework
Free money, technically
The three alpha layers explained, briefly

None of these require stock-picking genius, market timing, or a Bloomberg terminal. They require structure. FIRE Portal gives you the specific recommendations. You just have to follow them, which is the hard part, because inertia is powerful and brokerage interfaces are annoying.

Location Alpha
Put funds in the right accounts

Bonds in tax-deferred. Factor ETFs in Roth. Total market index in taxable. Wrong order = annual drag, every year, forever.

Worth 0.5%–1.5%/yr. Zero extra risk.
Tax Alpha
Know the dollar cost of inaction

The tool shows the cumulative dollar gap between your current allocation and the optimized one. It's a real number. It's probably uncomfortable. Good.

Often $100K–$300K over 20 years.
Estate Alpha
Your heirs get the step-up

Appreciated taxable assets passed to heirs reset cost basis at death. All embedded gains vanish. Tax-free. This changes how you sequence withdrawals.

All embedded capital gains: gone.
